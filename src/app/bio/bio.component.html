Introduction

Hi, my name is Luca DiMaggio. From the moment I picked up a camera, I felt an undeniable pull toward photography. What began as a simple hobby quickly transformed into a passionate pursuit that has shaped my life in profound ways. Looking back, my journey as a professional photographer has been a blend of creativity, technical mastery, and personal growth.

Initially, my interest in photography was sparked during family vacations, where I captured fleeting moments and stunning landscapes. As I shared these images with friends and family, their encouraging feedback ignited a desire to delve deeper. I began to study the fundamentals of photography, experimenting with different settings and techniques. I remember spending countless hours learning about the exposure triangle—ISO, aperture, and shutter speed—until I could manipulate them instinctively. This hands-on practice was vital, as it helped me develop a solid foundation.

As I honed my skills, I started to focus on various genres, but portrait photography quickly captured my heart. There's something incredibly rewarding about connecting with individuals and capturing their essence in a single frame. I remember my first portrait session with a friend; I was nervous, but as we worked together, I felt a creative synergy that was exhilarating. It taught me the importance of communication and trust, which are crucial when working with subjects.

Building my brand was a challenging yet exciting phase. I created a website and shared my work on social media, allowing my photography to reach a wider audience. Networking became essential, and I attended local photography events and workshops to meet other creatives. These connections not only provided invaluable insights but also fostered a sense of community that motivated me to keep pushing my limits.

As I transitioned into freelancing, I encountered the realities of running a photography business. It required balancing creativity with the practical aspects of pricing, contracts, and client relationships. I learned the importance of being organized and maintaining a professional demeanor, even when faced with challenges. For instance, there was a time when I experienced technical issues just before a big shoot. Instead of panicking, I remembered the troubleshooting skills I had developed over the years, which ultimately saved the day.

Continuous learning has been a cornerstone of my career. The photography world is ever-evolving, and staying current with new techniques and technology is essential. I often find myself attending workshops and online courses, eager to expand my knowledge. Recently, I dived into the world of post-processing, mastering software like Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop. This newfound skill has allowed me to elevate my images and bring my artistic vision to life.

The journey hasn't been without its challenges. There were moments of self-doubt, especially when comparing my work to others. However, I learned to embrace my unique style and focus on my growth rather than seeking validation. Each photograph is a reflection of my perspective, and I've come to appreciate the value of authenticity in my work.

As I reflect on my career thus far, I feel immense gratitude for the experiences and connections I've made. Photography has not only allowed me to express my creativity but has also taught me resilience and adaptability. Whether I'm capturing a wedding, a portrait, or a landscape, I approach each project with the same enthusiasm and dedication.

Ultimately, being a professional photographer is more than just taking pictures; it's about storytelling and connecting with people. Every image I capture holds a story, a moment in time that deserves to be remembered. As I continue this journey, I look forward to embracing new challenges and capturing the beauty of life through my lens.

Skills

  • Focus Stacking: Combining multiple images taken at different focus points for increased depth of field in macro photography.
  • Frequency Separation: Advanced skin retouching technique that separates texture and color for high-end retouching.
  • Color Grading Techniques: Using LUTs (Look-Up Tables) for consistent color grading across images
  • ICC Profiles: Understanding how to use ICC profiles for accurate color management in printing and display.
  • Digital Asset Management (DAM): Utilizing software like Adobe Bridge or Lightroom for efficient cataloging and tagging of images.
  • Seamless Backdrops: Techniques for setting up and using seamless paper backdrops for clean backgrounds.
  • Camera Rigging: Building and using camera rigs for stability in dynamic shooting situations (e.g., gimbals, sliders)
  • Drone Photography: Knowledge of aerial photography, including drone operation and safety regulations.

Background

  • 2010 - 2011: workshops at Les Photographies de Paris to master composition and lighting techniques, practicing in iconic locations like Montmartre.
  • 2012 - 2014: After making shoots for friends and family in various settings back in Rome, collaborating with local models via Model Mayhem to create a diverse portfolio, focusing on portrait and lifestyle photography.
  • 2015: Launches a professional website and builds a social media presence on Instagram, showcasing work from Amsterdam's canals and tulip fields.
  • 2016: Secures first paid gigs through platforms like Fiverr and Thumbtack, focusing on event photography for local festivals in Barcelona like La Mercè.
  • 2017 - 2019: Chooses to specialize in fashion photography, collaborating with local designers during Milan Fashion Week and taking courses at Istituto Europeo di Design (IED).
